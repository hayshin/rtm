\abstract{Clinical documentation imposes a substantial burden on primary care physicians, with patient--clinician consultations generating diagnostically rich spoken dialogue that remains largely inaccessible to automated health information systems in its raw form. This paper presents the design and evaluation of an automated six-step pipeline that transforms unstructured clinical audio recordings directly into validated HL7 FHIR Release 4B-compliant data structures without requiring custom model development or manual post-editing. The pipeline integrates acoustic pre-processing with voice activity detection, two-speaker diarization, automatic speech recognition using Medical-Whisper-Large-v3, large language model-driven transcript cleaning and physician/patient role assignment, joint clinical information extraction with schema-constrained FHIR bundle construction, and final validation with provenance tracking. On the 15 PriMock57 Day~1 consultations currently processed end-to-end, the system achieved a 100\% FHIR validation pass rate, a corpus-level Step~3 word error rate of 0.322, and a Step~4 speaker-role accuracy of 0.896. The transcript-cleaning stage improved readability but increased transcript-faithful WER to 0.595, indicating that it functions as a semantic normalization step rather than an ASR improvement stage. These results show that end-to-end audio-to-FHIR transformation is technically feasible on a public benchmark and can produce structurally valid, auditable HL7 FHIR R4B bundles, while also making clear that transcript fidelity remains the main bottleneck for downstream semantic reliability.}
